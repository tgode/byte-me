# Sick Leave Policy — Serbia

**Document:** Sick Leave Policy  
**Country:** Serbia  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026

---

## Sick Leave Entitlement

Sick leave in Serbia is regulated by the Law on Compulsory Social Insurance (Zakon o obaveznom socijalnom osiguranju).

- **Days 1–30 of illness:** The employer pays 65% of the employee's average salary (calculated over the preceding 12 months)
- **Days 31 and beyond:** The Health Insurance Fund (RFZO) covers 65% of the average salary

For work-related injuries or occupational diseases, the compensation rate is **100%** from day 1, paid by the employer and then reimbursed by RFZO.

---

## Notification Procedure

Employees must notify their direct manager or the HR department **before or at the start of the working day** in which the sick leave begins.

Notification may be given by phone, Teams message, or email.

---

## Medical Documentation

A sick leave certificate (doznaka) issued by a licensed physician or the National Health Insurance Fund (RFZO) is required for any sick leave period.

The doznaka must be submitted to HR within **5 working days** of the first day of absence.

---

## Extended Illness

For sick leave exceeding 60 days, HR will contact the employee to discuss accommodation options and document necessary arrangements.

---

## Return to Work

After sick leave exceeding 10 working days, a brief return-to-work discussion with the direct manager is recommended. A medical clearance certificate may be required in certain cases.
